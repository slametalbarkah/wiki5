Caroline Blazovsky

Caroline Blazovsky is a residential environmental consultant, nationally recognized as a healthy home expert, investigator, and entrepreneur. She works as a public educator and spokesperson in the home improvement industry. She is the founder of My Healthy Home, an organization focused on creating healthy living environments. She is a former co-host of a syndicated home improvement radio show and podcast at Talk Media Network.





== Education ==

Caroline Blazovsky attended Hunterdon Central Regional High School in Flemington, NJ. She then went on to Muhlenberg College in Allentown, PA to gain philosophy and political science degrees. She was an intern for the Howard Stern Show in New York City. Blazovsky has a graduate certification from the Boston Architectural College in sustainable design and studied Public Health at the University of Southern California.





== Career ==

Since May 21, 2003, Blazovsky has been the CEO and founder of My Healthy Home, a company that offers mold, mycotoxins, volatile organic compounds, allergens, formaldehyde, and other contaminants testing, indoor air testing, consultations and healthy home products.

Her company specializes in UV light disinfection technologies, dehumidification, and filtration products and is a certified green business through Green America. In 2020, her company was a finalist for NJBIZ Business of the Year 2020.
