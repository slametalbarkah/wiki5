Bolshoye Topolnoye

Bolshoye Topolnoye (Russian: Большое Топольное), meaning "big poplar", is a lake in Burlinsky District, Altai Krai, Russian Federation.

The lake lies at the northwestern end of the Krai. A small part of the northwestern shore belongs to the SW corner of Novosibirsk Oblast. There are no settlements by the lake. The nearest inhabited places are Petrovka and Mikhailovka.





== Geography ==

Bolshoye Topolnoye is one of the largest lakes in Altai Krai. It has an oval shape and is located in the Kulunda Plain, southern part of the West Siberian Plain. Its southwestern shore is barely 0.2 kilometers (0.12 mi) to the east of the Kazakhstan–Russia border. Its waters are slightly saline. The Burla river flows into the northeastern area of the lake.
